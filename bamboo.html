Bamboo Train (ឡូរី)

The Bamboo Train, locally known as "Norry," is one of Battambang's most unique and thrilling attractions. This ingenious form of transportation consists of a bamboo platform mounted on two sets of wheels, powered by a small gasoline engine. It's a one-of-a-kind experience that you won't find anywhere else in the world!

Fun Fact!

The Bamboo Train can reach speeds of up to 40 km/h (25 mph) as it rattles along the old French colonial railway tracks. Hold on tight!

How It Works

The Bamboo Train operates on a single railway track, which creates an interesting challenge: when two trains meet coming from opposite directions, one must be disassembled to let the other pass! The train with fewer passengers or lighter cargo is typically the one that gets taken apart. The whole process takes just a few minutes, as locals have perfected this routine over many years.

The Experience

The journey typically lasts about 30-40 minutes round trip, taking you through beautiful countryside scenery including rice paddies, small villages, and lush vegetation. The wind in your face, the rhythmic clatter of the wheels on the tracks, and the stunning rural landscapes make for an unforgettable adventure.

History

The Bamboo Train was born out of necessity during the 1980s when Cambodia's railway system had been destroyed during years of conflict. Locals created this innovative solution to transport people and goods along the unused tracks. What started as a practical solution has now become one of Cambodia's most popular tourist attractions.

Visitor Information

Location: O Dambong village, about 4 km from Battambang city
Duration: 30-40 minutes round trip
Best Time: Early morning or late afternoon to avoid the heat
Tip: Bring a camera and hold onto your belongings!
