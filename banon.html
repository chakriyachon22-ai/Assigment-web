Banon Temple (ប្រាសាទបាណន់)

Banon Temple is a stunning ancient Angkorian temple located in Battambang Province, Cambodia. Built in the 11th century during the reign of King Udayadityavarman II, this hilltop temple offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding countryside and rice paddies.

Quick Facts

Built: 11th century (around 1027 AD)
Location: 25 km south of Battambang city
Steps to climb: Approximately 358 steps
Architecture: Five towers in quincunx pattern

The temple sits atop a hill and requires climbing approximately 358 steps to reach the top. The journey is well worth the effort, as visitors are rewarded with spectacular views and well-preserved ancient architecture. The temple complex consists of five towers arranged in a quincunx pattern, similar to Angkor Wat.

Banon Temple is not only a significant historical site but also an active place of worship. Local monks maintain a pagoda at the base of the hill, and the site remains an important spiritual destination for Cambodian Buddhists. The peaceful atmosphere and scenic beauty make it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling through Battambang Province.

The best time to visit Banon Temple is early morning or late afternoon when the temperature is cooler and the lighting is perfect for photography. The sunset views from the top are particularly spectacular, with golden light illuminating the vast rice fields below.
